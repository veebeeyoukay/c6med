## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the framework for delivering consistent, effective security awareness training to all C6Med personnel, including new hires, existing employees, and contractors.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to:
- All new employees (W-2)
- All contractors (1099)
- Temporary staff
- Interns
- Third-party personnel with system access

### 1.3 Objectives
- Ensure all personnel receive appropriate security training
- Maintain compliance with client MSA requirements
- Support cyber insurance policy requirements
- Reduce security incidents through education
- Create a security-aware culture

---

## 2. Training Program Overview

### 2.1 Training Curriculum

C6Med's security training program consists of three primary components:

| Training Type | Audience | Frequency | Duration | Format |
|--------------|----------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Cybersecurity 25** | All personnel | New hire + Annual | 90 minutes | Live/Recorded |
| **Technical Implementation** | 5-6 key personnel | New hire + As needed | 4-6 hours | Hands-on |
| **Policy Acknowledgment** | All personnel | New hire + Annual | 30 minutes | Self-paced |

### 2.2 Training Timeline

#### New Hire Timeline:
- **Day 1:** Welcome and security overview (15 minutes)
- **Week 1:** Cybersecurity 25 training (90 minutes)
- **Week 1:** Policy acknowledgment and signatures (30 minutes)
- **Week 2:** Technical training (if applicable, 4-6 hours)
- **Week 2:** Validation and Q&A session (30 minutes)

#### Annual Timeline:
- **Q1 (January-March):** Annual refresher planning
- **Q2 (April-June):** Annual refresher delivery
- **Q3 (July-September):** Targeted training based on incidents
- **Q4 (October-December):** Training program review and updates

## 4. New Hire Security Training

### 4.1 Pre-Training Preparation

#### 4.1.1 Schedule Training (Done by HR/Onboarding Coordinator)
**Timeline:** Within 3 business days of hire date

**Steps:**
1. Add new hire to training calendar
2. Send calendar invitation for Cybersecurity 25 session
3. Determine if technical training is required (based on role)
4. Schedule technical training if applicable
5. Document scheduled dates in HR system

**Template Email:**
```
Subject: Welcome to C6Med - Security Training Schedule

Hi [Employee Name],

Welcome to C6Med! As part of your onboarding, you're required to complete security awareness training. Here's your schedule:

• Cybersecurity 25 Training
  Date: [Date]
  Time: [Time]
  Duration: 90 minutes
  Location/Link: [Zoom/Teams Link]

[If applicable:]
• Technical Implementation Training
  Date: [Date]
  Time: [Time]
  Duration: 4-6 hours
  Location/Link: [Zoom/Teams Link]

Please confirm your attendance. If you have conflicts, let me know ASAP.

Best regards,
[HR Coordinator Name]
```

#### 4.1.2 Prepare Training Materials (Done by Training Facilitator)
**Timeline:** 2 business days before training

**Steps:**
1. Verify training recording is accessible (if using recorded session)
2. Prepare participant handouts (print or digital)
3. Queue up presentation slides
4. Test audio/video equipment or conferencing platform
5. Prepare training roster for attendance tracking
6. Prepare quiz/assessment materials
7. Prepare certificate templates with participant names

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Presentation slides loaded and tested
- [ ] Participant handouts prepared (digital or print)
- [ ] Quiz materials ready
- [ ] Certificate templates prepared
- [ ] Zoom/Teams link tested
- [ ] Screen sharing capability verified
- [ ] Recording equipment tested (if recording new session)
- [ ] Attendance roster ready
- [ ] Feedback forms prepared

#### 4.1.3 Send Pre-Training Materials (Done by Training Facilitator or HR)
**Timeline:** 1-2 business days before training

**Materials to Send:**
- Training agenda
- Participant handout/quick reference guide
- Link to recorded training (if self-paced option available)
- Policy documents for review:
  - Information Security Policy
  - Acceptable Use Policy
  - Remote Work/BYOD Policy
  - Data Classification and Handling Policy

**Template Email:**
```
Subject: Upcoming Security Training - Pre-Reading Materials

Hi [Employee Name],

You're scheduled for Cybersecurity 25 training on [Date] at [Time].

Attached are pre-reading materials to help you get the most out of the training:
• Training agenda
• Quick reference guide
• Key policy documents

Please review these before the session. The training will be interactive, so come prepared with questions!

See you at the training!

Best regards,
[Training Facilitator Name]
```

### 4.2 Conducting Cybersecurity 25 Training

#### 4.2.1 Session Setup (15 minutes before start)
1. Start Zoom/Teams session
2. Enable recording (if applicable)
3. Test audio and video
4. Display welcome slide
5. Prepare attendance roster
6. Have handouts ready for distribution

#### 4.2.2 Training Delivery (90 minutes)
Follow the Cybersecurity 25 training script/facilitator guide:

**Timing Breakdown:**
- Introduction and objectives (5 minutes)
- Module 1-5 (60 minutes with breaks)
- Interactive exercises and discussions (15 minutes)
- Q&A session (10 minutes)

**Facilitation Tips:**
- Encourage questions throughout
- Use real-world pharmaceutical industry examples
- Relate content to C6Med's specific tools (M365, SharePoint, Dropbox)
- Keep pace engaging but not rushed
- Check for understanding regularly
- Note common questions for FAQ updates

#### 4.2.3 Training Assessment (15 minutes)
1. Distribute quiz/assessment
2. Allow 10 minutes for completion
3. Review answers and discuss (5 minutes)
4. Minimum passing score: 80% (12/15 correct)
5. If failed: Schedule remedial training

#### 4.2.4 Post-Training Activities (30 minutes after session)
1. Stop and save recording
2. Grade assessments
3. Prepare and distribute certificates
4. Collect feedback forms
5. Update training records
6. Send follow-up email with:
   - Training recording link (if available)
   - Training certificate
   - Quick reference materials
   - Resources and contacts

**Template Post-Training Email:**
```
Subject: Cybersecurity 25 Training - Certificate and Resources

Hi [Employee Name],

Thank you for completing the Cybersecurity 25 training today! Attached are:

• Your training completion certificate
• Quick reference guide (print and keep handy!)
• Training recording (for future reference)
• Contact information for security questions

Remember:
✓ Enable MFA on all accounts
✓ Use strong, unique passwords (consider 1Password)
✓ Think before you click
✓ Report suspicious activity immediately

If you have questions, contact: [Security Contact]

Best regards,
[Training Facilitator Name]
```

### 4.4 Technical Personnel Training

#### 4.4.1 Determine Training Need
**Criteria for Technical Training:**
- Role involves system administration
- Manages user access or permissions
- Responsible for security tool configuration
- Handles IT support or troubleshooting
- Manages backups or data protection

**Typically includes:**
- Owner/Leadership (Marcy Duval)
- IT coordinator(s)
- Key administrators
- 2-3 additional personnel (total 5-6)

#### 4.4.2 Schedule Technical Training
**Timeline:** Week 2-3 of employment (after Cybersecurity 25 completion)

**Options:**
- **Option A:** Full day session (6 hours with lunch)
- **Option B:** Two half-day sessions (3 hours each)
- **Option C:** Multiple 2-hour sessions over 2 weeks

**Recommended:** Option B (two half-days) for better retention

#### 4.4.3 Conduct Technical Training
Follow the Technical Implementation Training Guide:

**Day 1 Topics (3 hours):**
- Device management
- Microsoft 365 security configuration
- User access management
- MFA deployment

**Day 2 Topics (3 hours):**
- Password management
- Security monitoring
- Patch management
- Backup verification

**Format:**
- 40% instruction
- 60% hands-on exercises
- Lab environment for practice
- Real systems for specific configurations

#### 4.4.4 Hands-On Validation
At end of technical training:
1. Assign practical exercises
2. Observe completion of key tasks
3. Provide feedback and coaching
4. Document competencies achieved
5. Issue technical training certificate

**Validation Checklist:**
- [ ] Can enroll a device in management system
- [ ] Can provision a new user account
- [ ] Can configure MFA for a user
- [ ] Can review security logs
- [ ] Can verify backup status
- [ ] Can apply system updates
- [ ] Knows incident escalation procedures

## 5. Annual Refresher Training

### 5.1 Planning Annual Refresher

#### 5.1.1 Review and Update (Done in Q1)
**Timeline:** January-February each year

**Activities:**
1. Review previous year's security incidents
2. Assess training effectiveness metrics
3. Update training content based on:
   - New threats or attack vectors
   - New client requirements
   - Policy changes
   - Technology changes
   - Incident lessons learned
4. Review pharmaceutical industry trends
5. Update quiz questions
6. Refresh examples and scenarios

#### 5.1.2 Schedule Refresher Training
**Timeline:** Deliver in Q2 (April-June)

**Steps:**
1. Identify all personnel requiring training
2. Check training completion dates (>12 months?)
3. Schedule multiple sessions for flexibility:
   - Morning session (9:00 AM)
   - Afternoon session (2:00 PM)
   - Recording for async completion
4. Send calendar invitations 30 days in advance
5. Send reminders at 2 weeks and 1 week

**Template Invitation Email:**
```
Subject: Annual Security Refresher Training - Please Confirm Attendance

Hi Team,

It's time for our annual security refresher training! This year's training includes:
• Latest phishing and social engineering trends
• New MSA client requirements
• Lessons learned from 2025 incidents
• Updated policies and procedures

Session Options (choose one):
• Session A: [Date] at 9:00 AM (60 minutes)
• Session B: [Date] at 2:00 PM (60 minutes)
• Self-paced: Recording available by [Date]

Please RSVP by [Date]. Attendance is mandatory for all employees.

Questions? Contact [HR/Training Coordinator]

Best regards,
[Name]
```

### 5.2 Delivering Annual Refresher

#### 5.2.1 Refresher Content (60 minutes)
**Streamlined version of Cybersecurity 25:**
- Brief review of fundamentals (10 minutes)
- Focus on "What's New" (30 minutes):
  - Recent threat trends
  - Company-specific incidents or near-misses
  - Policy updates
  - New tools or procedures
- Interactive discussion (10 minutes)
- Updated quiz (10 minutes)

#### 5.2.2 Make It Engaging
- Use real C6Med examples (anonymized incidents)
- Incorporate current events (recent healthcare breaches)
- Share success stories (caught phishing attempts)
- Interactive polls and quizzes
- Prizes or recognition for participation

#### 5.2.3 Assessment and Certification
1. Administer updated quiz (10 questions)
2. Minimum passing score: 80%
3. Issue updated certificate (note year)
4. Document completion in training tracker

### 5.3 Tracking Refresher Completion

**Methods:**
- Training tracker spreadsheet
- HR system flags
- Calendar reminders for upcoming due dates
- Monthly reporting to leadership

**Escalation for Non-Completion:**
- Week 1: Friendly reminder email
- Week 2: Manager notification
- Week 3: Formal notice from HR/Security Manager
- Week 4: Escalation to leadership

## 6. Technical Personnel Training

### 6.1 Ongoing Technical Training

Technical personnel should receive:
- **Annual refresher** on all technical topics (4 hours)
- **Quarterly updates** on new tools or procedures (1 hour)
- **Ad-hoc training** when implementing new systems

### 6.2 Technical Skills Maintenance

#### 6.2.1 Quarterly Technical Reviews (1 hour each)
**Topics rotation:**
- Q1: Incident response and forensics updates
- Q2: New threat vectors and defenses
- Q3: Compliance and audit preparation
- Q4: Disaster recovery and business continuity

#### 6.2.2 Hands-On Practice
Conduct quarterly:
- Tabletop exercises (incident response scenarios)
- Backup restoration tests
- User provisioning/deprovisioning drills
- Security tool configuration reviews

### 6.3 External Training Opportunities

**Recommended:**
- Annual security conference (e.g., RSAC, Black Hat, local InfoSec events)
- Vendor-specific training (Microsoft, 1Password, etc.)
- Industry webinars and online courses
- Professional certifications (Security+, CISSP, etc.) - if budget allows

**Budget allocation:** Consider $1,000-2,000 per technical person annually

## 7. Training Delivery Methods

### 7.1 Delivery Options

| Method | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| **Live In-Person** | High engagement, immediate Q&A | Scheduling challenges | Kickoff, technical training |
| **Live Virtual** | Flexible, can record | Technical issues possible | Most training scenarios |
| **Recorded/Self-Paced** | Flexible timing | Lower engagement | Refreshers, async learners |
| **Hybrid** | Flexibility + engagement | Complex coordination | Large groups |

### 7.2 Platform Recommendations

**Video Conferencing:**
- Primary: Zoom or Microsoft Teams
- Features needed: Recording, breakout rooms, polls, screen sharing

**Learning Management System (LMS):**
- For growing company: Consider simple LMS (TalentLMS, Lessonly)
- Current: Use SharePoint for document management

**Assessment Tools:**
- Current: Google Forms or Microsoft Forms
- Alternative: Quiz tools in LMS if adopted

### 7.3 Recording and Archiving

**Recording Guidelines:**
1. Always record live training sessions
2. Edit for clarity (remove dead air, technical issues)
3. Add captions/subtitles
4. Store on SharePoint with restricted access
5. Maintain for at least 3 years (audit/compliance)

**Storage Structure:**

## 10. Training Effectiveness Assessment

### 10.1 Measuring Effectiveness

**Quantitative Metrics:**
- **Training completion rate:** Target 100%
- **Average quiz scores:** Target ≥85%
- **Time to completion:** Track for new hires
- **Phishing simulation click rates:** Expect decrease post-training
- **Security incident rates:** Track pre/post training
- **Policy violations:** Track frequency and type

**Qualitative Metrics:**
- Participant feedback ratings (1-5 scale)
- Open-ended feedback comments
- Manager observations of behavior change
- Cultural indicators (proactive reporting, security questions)

### 10.2 Feedback Collection

#### 10.2.1 Post-Training Survey (Immediate)
Distribute after every training session:

**Sample Questions (1-5 scale):**
1. The training content was relevant to my role
2. The training was engaging and well-paced
3. The facilitator was knowledgeable and clear
4. I understand how to apply what I learned
5. The training materials were helpful

**Open-Ended:**
- What was most valuable about this training?
- What could be improved?
- What topics need more coverage?
- Additional comments or questions?

#### 10.2.2 Follow-Up Survey (30 days post-training)
**Sample Questions:**
1. Have you applied what you learned in the training?
2. Have you encountered situations where the training was helpful?
3. Are there gaps in your knowledge you'd like addressed?
4. Do you feel confident in your security responsibilities?

### 10.3 Continuous Improvement

**Quarterly Training Program Review:**
1. Review all feedback collected
2. Analyze metrics and trends
3. Identify improvement opportunities
4. Implement changes for next quarter
5. Document improvements made

**Annual Program Assessment:**
1. Comprehensive review of all aspects
2. Benchmark against industry standards
3. Budget review and planning
4. Set goals for next year
5. Update this SOP as needed
