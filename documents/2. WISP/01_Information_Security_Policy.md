## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting C6Med LLC's information assets, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data critical to our pharmaceutical medical communications services. This policy supports our commitment to safeguarding client information, particularly sensitive pharmaceutical data from Novartis, Novo Nordisk, Regeneron, and other clients.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All C6Med employees (W-2), contractors, and third-party service providers
- All information systems, applications, and data owned or managed by C6Med
- All devices used to access C6Med resources, including personal devices under our BYOD program
- All locations where C6Med business is conducted, including home offices

### 1.3 Framework Alignment
This policy aligns with:
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 (CSF 2.0)
- Pharmaceutical industry MSA security requirements
- Cyber insurance underwriting requirements
- Texas state data protection regulations

## 2. Information Security Objectives

C6Med is committed to:
- **Confidentiality**: Protecting sensitive pharmaceutical and client information from unauthorized disclosure
- **Integrity**: Ensuring accuracy and completeness of information throughout its lifecycle
- **Availability**: Maintaining reliable access to information and systems for authorized users
- **Compliance**: Meeting contractual, regulatory, and industry requirements
- **Risk Management**: Identifying and mitigating information security risks appropriately

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

### 3.1 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Ultimate accountability for information security at C6Med
- Approve security policies and major security investments
- Ensure adequate resources for security initiatives
- Review security program effectiveness annually

### 3.2 Security Coordinator (Designated Employee)
- Coordinate day-to-day security activities
- Maintain security documentation and policies
- Coordinate security awareness training
- Serve as primary contact for security incidents
- Manage relationships with security vendors and consultants

### 3.3 All Employees
- Comply with all security policies and procedures
- Protect company and client data according to classification levels
- Report security incidents immediately
- Complete required security awareness training
- Maintain security of personal devices used for work (BYOD)

### 3.4 Third-Party Service Providers
- Comply with C6Med security requirements as specified in contracts
- Report security incidents affecting C6Med data
- Maintain appropriate security certifications and insurance

## 4. Core Security Principles

### 4.1 Defense in Depth
Implement multiple layers of security controls to protect against various threat vectors.

### 4.2 Least Privilege
Grant users only the minimum access rights necessary to perform their job functions.

### 4.3 Separation of Duties
Distribute critical functions among multiple individuals where feasible.

### 4.4 Privacy by Design
Integrate privacy and data protection considerations into all business processes.

### 4.5 Continuous Improvement
Regularly review and enhance security measures based on emerging threats and lessons learned.

## 5. Security Framework Components

### 5.1 Governance (NIST CSF GV)
- Maintain current security policies and procedures
- Define clear roles and responsibilities
- Establish risk management processes
- Ensure compliance with legal and contractual requirements

### 5.2 Risk Management (NIST CSF ID.RA)
- Conduct annual risk assessments
- Maintain asset inventory
- Identify and document threats and vulnerabilities
- Implement risk treatment plans

### 5.3 Access Control (NIST CSF PR.AC)
- Enforce identity verification and authentication
- Manage access permissions based on roles
- Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for critical systems
- Review access rights quarterly

### 5.4 Data Security (NIST CSF PR.DS)
- Classify data according to sensitivity
- Encrypt sensitive data in transit and at rest
- Implement secure disposal procedures
- Maintain data backup and recovery capabilities

### 5.5 Security Awareness (NIST CSF PR.AT)
- Provide initial security training for new employees
- Conduct annual security awareness training
- Communicate security updates and threats regularly
- Test employee security awareness through simulated exercises

### 5.6 Protective Technology (NIST CSF PR.PT)
- Deploy and maintain endpoint protection on all devices
- Implement security monitoring where feasible
- Maintain security configurations
- Control removable media usage

### 5.7 Incident Response (NIST CSF RS)
- Maintain incident response plan
- Establish incident reporting procedures
- Conduct post-incident reviews
- Implement lessons learned

### 5.8 Recovery (NIST CSF RC)
- Maintain business continuity plans
- Test recovery procedures annually
- Ensure backup integrity and availability
- Document recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO)

## 6. Policy Compliance

### 6.1 Compliance Monitoring
- Security Coordinator reviews compliance quarterly
- CEO reviews security program annually
- External assessments conducted as required by clients or insurance

### 6.2 Non-Compliance
Non-compliance with this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warnings
- Additional training requirements
- Access privilege restrictions
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Legal action for serious breaches

### 6.3 Exceptions
- Policy exceptions must be documented and approved by the CEO
- Exceptions are reviewed quarterly and must include compensating controls
- Temporary exceptions cannot exceed 90 days without re-approval

## 9. Definitions

**Information Asset**: Any data, system, or resource that has value to C6Med
**Personal Device**: Employee-owned device used for work under BYOD program
**Sensitive Data**: Information classified as Confidential or Restricted
**Security Incident**: Any event that compromises confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information
