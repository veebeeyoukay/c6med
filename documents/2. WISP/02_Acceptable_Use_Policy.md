## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines appropriate use of C6Med LLC's information systems, devices, and data to protect our pharmaceutical clients' sensitive information and maintain the security and integrity of our technology resources. This policy ensures professional conduct and regulatory compliance while supporting productive remote work.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All C6Med employees, contractors, and third parties
- All company-provided and personal devices used for C6Med business (BYOD)
- All C6Med information systems, including Microsoft Office 365, SharePoint, Dropbox, and Teams
- All network connections and internet usage for business purposes
- All data accessed, processed, or stored for C6Med business

### 1.3 NIST CSF Alignment
This policy supports the following NIST CSF 2.0 controls:
- PR.AC-1: Identities and credentials are managed for authorized devices and users
- PR.AT-1: All users are informed and trained
- PR.DS-1: Data-at-rest is protected
- PR.IP-1: A baseline configuration is created and maintained

## 2. General Use Guidelines

### 2.1 Professional Use
- Information systems are provided primarily for business purposes
- Limited personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work responsibilities
- All use must maintain C6Med's professional reputation with pharmaceutical clients
- Users must not engage in activities that could damage C6Med's relationship with Novartis, Novo Nordisk, Regeneron, or other clients

### 2.2 Pharmaceutical Industry Compliance
Users must:
- Protect all pharmaceutical client data according to MSA requirements
- Never share or discuss client proprietary information outside authorized channels
- Maintain strict confidentiality of drug development and medical communications materials
- Report any potential compliance violations immediately

## 3. Acceptable Use

### 3.1 Authorized Activities
Users MAY:
- Access systems and data necessary for assigned job functions
- Use company-approved collaboration tools (Teams, SharePoint, Dropbox) for business purposes
- Store work files in designated secure locations
- Use internet resources for legitimate business research
- Participate in approved training and professional development
- Conduct limited personal activities that don't violate other policies

### 3.2 Required Security Practices
Users MUST:
- Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) where available
- Lock devices when unattended (even in home offices)
- Use only approved software and cloud services for work
- Keep devices and software updated with security patches
- Report suspicious emails or activities immediately
- Encrypt sensitive data before transmission
- Use secure Wi-Fi connections (WPA2/WPA3) for remote work

## 4. Unacceptable Use

### 4.1 Prohibited Activities
Users MUST NOT:
- Access or attempt to access systems without authorization
- Share login credentials or authentication tokens
- Disable or bypass security controls
- Install unauthorized software or browser extensions
- Connect unauthorized devices to company systems
- Use company resources for illegal activities
- Download or distribute copyrighted material without permission
- Access inappropriate content (pornographic, discriminatory, etc.)
- Conduct unauthorized business activities or side work
- Modify system configurations without approval

### 4.2 Data Handling Violations
Users MUST NOT:
- Store client data on unapproved personal cloud services
- Email sensitive data to personal email accounts
- Share confidential information via unencrypted channels
- Leave sensitive documents visible during video calls
- Discuss client projects in public spaces
- Post about client work on social media
- Use client data for any purpose other than assigned work
- Retain client data after project completion without authorization

### 4.3 Communication Restrictions
Users MUST NOT:
- Send messages that could be considered harassment or discrimination
- Impersonate others or misrepresent C6Med
- Send bulk unsolicited emails (spam)
- Forward chain letters or pyramid schemes
- Share false or misleading information
- Engage in activities that violate client communication policies

## 5. Email and Messaging Guidelines

### 5.1 Email Use
- Use professional tone and language in all communications
- Include appropriate confidentiality notices for sensitive information
- Verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive data
- Use encryption for highly sensitive communications
- Archive important emails according to retention policies
- Report suspicious emails without clicking links or attachments

### 5.2 Microsoft Teams and Collaboration Tools
- Keep client discussions in designated private channels
- Do not share client files in public or general channels
- Use appropriate channels for different types of communication
- Maintain professional conduct in all messages and calls
- Do not record meetings without participant consent

## 6. Internet and Social Media

### 6.1 Internet Usage
- Access only legitimate business and approved personal sites
- Do not download software from untrusted sources
- Avoid peer-to-peer file sharing applications
- Use corporate VPN when accessing sensitive resources from public networks
- Report any security warnings or blocked sites to Security Coordinator

### 6.2 Social Media Guidelines
- Do not disclose confidential or proprietary information
- Do not mention clients or projects without approval
- Maintain professional representation of C6Med
- Clarify that personal opinions are your own
- Follow pharmaceutical industry social media guidelines

## 7. Device Management (BYOD)

### 7.1 Personal Device Requirements
When using personal devices for work:
- Enable device encryption (BitLocker, FileVault, etc.)
- Use passcode/biometric lock with automatic timeout (5 minutes)
- Keep operating system and applications updated
- Install approved antivirus/anti-malware software
- Allow remote wipe capability for company data if required
- Separate work and personal data where possible

### 7.2 Data Protection on Personal Devices
- Store work files only in approved cloud services
- Do not save sensitive data to local device storage
- Use company-approved applications for work tasks
- Regularly delete cached data and temporary files
- Backup important work data to approved locations

## 8. Monitoring and Privacy

### 8.1 Monitoring Notice
C6Med reserves the right to monitor:
- Email and messaging communications
- Internet usage and access logs
- File access and modifications
- System login attempts and activities
- Compliance with security policies

### 8.2 Privacy Expectations
- Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company systems
- Personal use of systems may be subject to monitoring
- Monitoring is conducted for security, compliance, and business purposes
- Any illegal activities discovered will be reported to authorities

## 9. Compliance and Enforcement

### 9.1 Compliance Requirements
All users must:
- Acknowledge and agree to this policy before system access
- Complete annual security awareness training
- Report violations or suspicious activities immediately
- Cooperate with security investigations

### 9.2 Violation Consequences
Violations may result in:
- First offense: Written warning and additional training
- Second offense: Temporary suspension of system access
- Severe/repeated violations: Termination of employment
- Criminal violations: Report to law enforcement
- Client data breaches: Notification per contractual requirements

### 9.3 Reporting Violations
Report violations to:
- Primary: Security Coordinator
- Secondary: CEO
- Anonymous reporting: [Establish anonymous reporting mechanism]

- Understanding that violations may result in disciplinary action
- Agreeing to report suspected violations
- Understanding monitoring may occur
